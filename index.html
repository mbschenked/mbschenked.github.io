About Me

I'm a gameplay designer focused on technical combat systems, narrative experience design, and the intersection between player expression and mechanical depth. My work spans solo personal projects and collaborative team productions.

My background in chemical engineering gives me a systems-thinking lens — I approach game design the same way I'd approach a reaction network: isolate variables, iterate on parameters, and let emergent complexity arise from simple, well-defined rules.

I build primarily in Unreal Engine, leveraging modular, spreadsheet- integrated systems for rapid iteration. My design philosophy centers on momentum, adaptability, and thematic grounding.

Personal Project — 2025

Threads of God
Combat Demo

A third-person action combat demo built in Unreal Engine 5 — emphasizing fast-paced precision swordplay, fluid movement, and visual storytelling through a modular, data-driven combat architecture.

Unreal Engine 5 Solo Developer Combat Systems Combat Puzzles ▶ Watch on YouTube

"Momentum, adaptability, and thematic grounding inform every boss encounter — dynamic behavior trees and weighted action probability ensure no two fights feel the same."

Core Systems

Each system is modular and spreadsheet-integrated for streamlined iteration and scaling.

⚡

Combo System

Chained light and heavy attack sequences with movement-adaptive inputs and weapon-specific behaviour trees. Designed for expressive chaining without sacrificing readability.

🛡

Deflection System

Frame-precise deflection windows trigger QTE-driven counter sequences and finisher states. Rewards skilled play with spectacular visual payoff.

🔄

Stat & State System

Data-driven player attribute tables govern hit reactions, stagger thresholds, and stamina gating — all exposed to designers via spreadsheet for rapid tuning.

🏃

Locomotion System

8-directional state machine (Locomotion2H) driven by speed, movement angle, and weapon stance. Covers idle turn-in-place, jog start/stop, guard strafe, aim locomotion, and multi-tier jump landings — all blended with orientation warping and procedural foot IK for seamless transitions.

VFS Team Project — 2025

The Broken Hero

A single-player 3D fixed-camera platformer about a Greek soldier reborn from an ancient fresco — navigating a crumbling temple while switching between a physical 3D world and 2D fresco spaces that transform the player into an illustration.

Players traverse upward through a collapsing temple, hunted by a mythic Minotaur. Entering fresco artworks shifts gameplay from 3D exploration to side-scrolling 2D platforming — a dual-world mechanic that transforms both the visual language and the rules of movement.

As the player climbs, fragmented narrative elements surface — revealing the protagonist's history and building toward a final escape at the temple summit.
